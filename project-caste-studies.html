Critical Caste Studies

This research brings a critical caste studies lens to the field of social computing, examining how caste-based hierarchies and discrimination are replicated, amplified, and sometimes challenged in digital spaces. My work analyzes how dominant caste narratives are constructed and maintained on platforms like YouTube, and how caste dynamics manifest within the gig economy. A central goal is to develop new methodological approaches within HCI and CSCW to recognize and address caste in technology design, ensuring that digital futures are not built on centuries-old structures of inequality.

Sub-Projects

Critical Caste and Technology Studies

This work focuses on developing and adapting research methods to effectively study and surface the logic of caste operating within digital economy, platforms and online communities.

Caste and Education Policy

Examines how educational policies and access to education are shaped by caste-based graded hierarchy, particularly in the context of state responses and the digital divide.
