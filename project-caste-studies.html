Critical Caste Studies

This research brings a critical caste studies lens to the field of technology and social computing, examining how caste-based graded hierarchies and caste-pride are reproduced, amplified, and sometimes challenged in digital spaces. It examines how caste-privileged groups construct and sustain their narratives on platforms such as YouTube, and how caste relations are embedded within the gig economy. A central goal is to develop new methodological approaches within HCI and CSCW to recognize and address caste in technology design, ensuring that digital futures are not built on centuries-old structures of oppression.

Sub-Projects

Critical Caste and Technology Studies

This work focuses on developing and adapting research methods to effectively study and surface the logic of caste operating within digital economy, platforms and online communities.

Caste and Education Policy

Examines how educational policies and access to education are shaped by caste-based graded hierarchy, particularly in the context of state responses and the digital divide.
