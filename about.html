About Ashique Ali Thuppilikkat

PhD Candidate in Information Studies, University of Toronto

I am a PhD candidate at the Faculty of Information, University of Toronto, Canada, advised by Prof. Priyank Chandra. My doctoral research examines the role of technology in worker resistance and unionization within the context of platformized urban life. I investigate how digital tools can empower unions and workers on location-based labor platforms to organize and address the impacts of climate change on their working conditions.

My research interests span worker-centric platform designs, tech-mediated resistance in social movements, and critical studies of caste and technology, with a regional focus on South Asia. I am a Connaught PhD Fellow for Public Impact Scholarship (2025) and a member of the Street Lab at the University of Toronto, where I collaborate on projects exploring digital labor, solidarity economies, and social justice.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I engaged in public-facing scholarship, contributing to outlets like Jacobin, The Conversation, and Indian Cultural Forum, where I wrote about social movements, digital propaganda, and cooperative models in India. My work bridges academic research and public engagement, aiming to amplify marginalized voices in the platform economy.
