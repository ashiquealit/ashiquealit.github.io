Ashique Ali Thuppilikkat

PhD Student | University of Toronto | Labour & Social Computing

Ashique Ali Thuppilikkat (he/him) is a PhD student at the Faculty of Information, University of Toronto, Canada and a researcher at STREET Lab. His research focuses on the role of technology in worker resistance and unionisation amidst the platformization of urban life. Specifically, he examines how digital tools can support unions and workers on location-based digital labour platforms to organise and resist the impacts of algorithmic management and environmental hazards on labour practices.

About Me

He is a recipient of the 2025–26 Connaught PhDs for Public Impact Fellowship and the 2024 SDGs@UofT Student Award, and a former School of Cities Graduate Fellow. Ashique is also the co-founder of SAFAR, a research-action centre based in West Bengal, India.

Research Projects

Technology, Design & Labour Resistance

Investigating the role of digital tools in worker organization, unionization, and resistance against algorithmic management.

Technology & Social Movements

Examining how technology is used by and against social movements, with a focus on diasporic communities and digital propaganda.

Critical Caste Studies in Social Computing

Analyzing the reproduction of caste hierarchies in digital spaces and the potential for technology to challenge them.

Solidarity Economy

Exploring cooperative models and digital tools that foster economic and social solidarity.
